Gerald R. Ford

38th President of the United States (1974-1977)

About Gerald Ford

Gerald Ford became president in 1974 after the resignation of Richard Nixon, aiming to restore trust in the government.

Presidential Policies

  • Pardoning Richard Nixon
  • Economic stabilization
  • Helsinki Accords on human rights

Ford's Legacy

Ford is remembered for his integrity, efforts in healing the nation post-Watergate, and commitment to transparency.
